# Foundation

## Positioning Statement

**PrismaX is a robotics-native service layer that sets the standards for how robots, data, and human work are deployed to advance intelligence for physical AI systems.**

PrismaX operates human-in-the-loop systems where people and robots work together in real environments. Through these systems, PrismaX defines how robotic work is performed, how data is generated, and how that data contributes to intelligent behavior. By setting deployment standards, PrismaX enables intelligence to advance without requiring every team to solve the same problems from scratch.

PrismaX functions as a protocol for real-world robotic deployment, using an owner-operator model where humans deploy and supervise robots in live environments. Standards emerge from how these systems are operated, learned from, and reused across deployments.

---

## Category Definition — The Service Layer for Physical AI

Physical AI does not progress through models alone. Progress depends on how robots are deployed, how humans remain in the loop, and how real-world interaction is translated into usable intelligence.

The service layer for physical AI refers to the systems that:

- Define how robots are deployed for specific outcomes
- Standardize how real-world interaction data is generated
- Integrate human judgement where models fall short
- Turn fragmented robotics capability into deployable systems

PrismaX defines this category by operating live, human-in-the-loop systems and using them to establish standards for deployment, data collection, and learning across the robotics ecosystem.

In this context, the service layer for physical AI functions as a protocol: a shared set of deployment, data-generation, and human-in-the-loop standards that allow learning to compound across systems instead of resetting with each new robot or team.

---

## The Core Problem — Robotics Has Assets, Not Systems

Across the robotics ecosystem, valuable components already exist:

- Research labs generating meaningful data
- Robot manufacturers shipping increasingly capable hardware, often with the ability to collect data through deployment
  - Many already run internal validation loops to test performance, generate training data, and validate new capabilities. These efforts create meaningful data and can be a revenue stream, but they are difficult to scale consistently without shared standards and infrastructures.
- Companies acquiring robots without clear deployment paths
- Communities eager to contribute labor and expertise

What's missing is a shared way to deploy these assets effectively.

Without standards for form factors, sensors, tasks, and human involvement, robotics efforts remain fragmented. In practice, this includes consistency in how robots perceive the world, how they are manipulated, and how human input is captured, so data can be reused, compared, and learned from across systems. Hardware is misapplied, data is collected inconsistently, and intelligence fails to compound.

**PrismaX exists to turn disconnected robotics capabilities into deployable systems.**

---

## Why Now

Human-in-the-loop systems are no longer experimental. They are foundational to how physical AI is trained, validated, and deployed.

At the same time:

- Robotics hardware has become more accessible
- Demand for real-world data from AI and robotics companies has increased
- Capital is flowing into robotics without clear deployment standards
- Communities want to participate but lack tooling and guidance

PrismaX sits at the intersection of these shifts, providing the service layer that allows robots, people, and data to be deployed correctly from the start.

---

## How PrismaX Fits

PrismaX operates as a service layer connecting:

- **Robots** across different form factors and environments
- **Humans** who provide judgement, intervention, and skill
- **Data** generated through intentional, real-world operation driven by real demand
- **Intelligence** that improves as systems learn from deployment

By running live systems and observing what works in practice, PrismaX establishes protocol-level standards for how physical AI is deployed. These standards emerge from real operation, often through owner-operator workflows, and allow learning to compound instead of resetting with every new robot, team, or project.
